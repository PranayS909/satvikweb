Jackfruit Curry
Ingredients:
  • Jackfruit (unripe)
  • Bengal gram
  • Oil
  • Potato
  • Spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, red chili powder, garam masala)
  • Ginger
  • Bay leaves, dry red chilies
  • Salt and sugar
Fact about dish: The unripe jackfruit used in curry has a fibrous, meaty texture that closely resembles shredded chicken, making it a popular plant-based alternative in vegetarian and vegan dishes. This texture allows it to absorb spices and flavors well, enhancing the curry's richness.
Steps:
  1. Cut the jackfruit into medium-sized pieces. Soak the pieces in water with a pinch of turmeric for 15-20 minutes to reduce the sap, making it easier to handle. Soak the bengal gram for at least an hour, then grind it into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or kadai. Take small portions of the bengal gram paste and deep fry them to make pakoras. Fry until golden and set aside.
  3. Boil the jackfruit pieces in a pressure cooker for just 1 whistle and then remove.
  4. Heat oil in a pan or kadai. Sauté the boiled jackfruit pieces and potato cubes with salt and a pinch of turmeric over medium flame. Once done, remove from the flame and set aside. In the same pan, heat oil and add dry red chilies. Sauté until they darken. Add bay leaves, sauté, and then add paanchphoron. Let it splatter. Add grated ginger and sauté. Then add coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Sauté until the spices blend and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Add the sautéed jackfruit and potato mixture to the spice mix and combine well. Add salt and sugar, mix, sprinkle water, and sauté for 3-4 minutes on low flame. Add water to reach the desired gravy consistency and bring to a boil. Add garam masala.
  6. Add the fried bengal gram fritters and cook for another 2-3 minutes with the lid on.
  7. Simmer for 1 more minute and then switch off the flame.
